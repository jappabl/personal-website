## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Not AI slop overall** — the Schematic concept (technical-drawing grid, A1–A4 coordinate "sheets", crop marks, title block, contour-topo canvas, a real typeable console, Konami assembly mode) is a committed, personal POV. Someone would ask "how was this made," not "which AI made this."

The tells that remain:
- **Space Grotesk** is on the reflex-reject font list and the detector flags it as overused. It's the one generic choice in an otherwise distinctive system.
- **Em-dashes** (detector: 10) in copy/date ranges — house-style avoids them.
- **Numbered markers** (detector): here the A1–A4 coordinates *carry meaning* (sheets read in order) — defensible as voice, not reflex. Keep.
- Detector "single font" is a **false positive** (body is JetBrains Mono, headings Space Grotesk — two families).

## What's Working

1. **A real point of view.** The blueprint system is coherent top to bottom and unmistakably personal. The giant `HAO·LIN` with the red registration dot is a confident, memorable hero.
2. **Robust, honest motion.** Reveals are transform-only with opacity always 1, so nothing ever ships invisible (JS-off, reduced-motion, or headless). Reduced-motion is handled throughout. The contour backdrop renders once (static) — calm and cheap, not a constant rAF.
3. **Signatures with payoff.** A working console (`help`, `goto`, `cv`, `bg`, hidden `art`, `play` Snake) and the Konami assembly-mode egg deliver the "developer" personality without a paragraph explaining it.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Faint micro-labels fail contrast.** `--faint #9c9ca6` ≈ **2.7:1** on white (below even 3:1), used pervasively: nav descriptions, section meta, fact labels, project years, status bar, scroll cue, title-block labels. Confirmed washed-out in the render. The red `--signal #e5372a` ≈ **3.4:1** is also used for *small* text (A1–A4 coordinates, `.pn`, resume periods) — under 4.5:1. **Fix:** darken `--faint` to ≥4.5:1 (≈ `#6b6b73`); use a darker red (or ≥18px) for any red text; reserve ultra-light strictly for non-text decoration. → `/impeccable colorize`

**[P1] Expandable rows + console are mouse-only.** `.part .row` (project expand) and `.console .bar` (toggle) are clickable `<div>`s with no `tabindex`/role/keyboard handler/`aria-expanded`. Project specs and repo links can't be reached by keyboard or screen reader. **Fix:** make them real `<button>`s with Enter/Space + `aria-expanded`/`aria-controls`; add a visible `:focus-visible` ring. → `/impeccable harden`

**[P2] The portfolio's content is placeholder.** Projects (Latticework / Halfstep / Drift / Plumb) are carried-over template work; resume has "Example Labs"; LinkedIn is generic `in/haolin`. A portfolio whose *selected work* is fake undercuts its whole purpose. **Fix:** drop in real projects (name, one-liner, stack, year, link), real roles, and the real LinkedIn. (Content, but the highest-stakes gap.)

**[P2] Space Grotesk + over-ceiling hero.** The display face is the reflex default; consider a more characterful technical/grotesque to match the POV (keep JetBrains Mono — mono is *earned* for this technical brand). The hero caps at **184px** (house ceiling ~96px) — defensible as a statement, but combined with the dense tiny-uppercase annotations it tips toward shouting + busy. → `/impeccable typeset`

**[P3] Copy + dead code.** 10 em-dashes (swap for en-dashes in ranges / restructure). `.crosshair` CSS is vestigial (the JS no longer activates it). No `PRODUCT.md`/`DESIGN.md` (removed in the rebuild) to anchor future work. → `/impeccable clarify` + `/impeccable polish`

## Persona Red Flags

**Sam (accessibility):** the faint gray + small red text fail WCAG AA; project expand and console open are unreachable without a mouse; no enhanced focus ring. (Credit: reduced-motion and JS-off are handled well.)

**Jordan (first-timer):** the blueprint jargon (BILL OF MATERIALS, X:04, FIG.1, SHEET 1/4) is charming but cryptic, and the washed-out labels make the wayfinding text hard to read. They'll still find Projects/Resume/About/Contact.

**Casey (mobile):** layout adapts well (crosshair hidden, console resizes, ledger collapses), but the low-contrast labels are even harder on a phone in daylight, and the docked console can sit over content until minimized.

## Minor Observations

- The contour backdrop is tasteful and the white text-halo keeps dark text crisp — the background is *not* the problem; the faint label color is.
- Hidden `bg contour|dots|grid` command is a nice touch; fine to leave as an easter egg.
- Scroll-cue shimmer loops infinitely (off under reduced-motion) — fine.

## Questions to Consider

- What are your *real* projects and roles? The work section is the portfolio.
- Is the blueprint metaphor density (coordinates, "BILL OF MATERIALS") earning its keep, or is it cleverness that costs legibility?
- Is the 184px hero the statement you want, or would ~110px read more refined?
